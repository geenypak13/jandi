About

Hi. I’m Jey Park —
I run a one-person company
out of Yangpyeong, Korea.

What I do

I’m an “AI orchestra conductor.” I direct a range of AI tools to build the app and website that fit one specific business owner.

How I work

I don’t sell fixed templates. I go to your shop and watch how you actually work.

At Yangjimal Charcoal Grill, I saw the staff signing in on paper — and proposed an app right there on the spot. That’s how a tool made only for that one shop comes to be.

Why you can trust me

I’ve built and run a staff attendance app, a stock-tracking web app, and a Toss-payment-integrated web app — all myself.

The app, the data, the accounts are all handed over to you (BYOC). I’m the one who builds it for you — not someone who holds onto your information.

Honestly

I do this because I find it genuinely fun. That’s exactly why I want to keep getting better at it.

I won’t pretend to be perfect. What I will do is think it through right beside you, all the way to the end.

Why I started

At first, I built apps just for myself. As I studied AI and made one tool after another, a thought struck me: the gap between people who can personalize their own work with AI and those who can’t is only going to grow.

But I got stuck on “how do I turn this into a real business?” — and burned out. Just to get out of my own head and move my body, I applied, on a whim, to a part-time job listing I happened to come across.

On my first day, I watched the owner check ten employees’ clock-in times one by one in Excel. “Want me to turn this into a simple app for you?” — that one sentence was the beginning.

Building something to solve someone else’s problem instead of my own, I realized: I’m someone who loves taking other people’s repetitive headaches and making them simple, with AI.

Even when it wouldn’t come together and I was stuck on it for days, it never felt like stress to me — it was a puzzle I enjoyed. So I decided what I want to do with my life. Right now, I’m drawing up that blueprint.

Not sure where to start? That’s completely fine — we sort it out together.
